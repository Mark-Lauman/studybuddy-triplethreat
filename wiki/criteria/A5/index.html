Assignment 5: Version 3

This assignment requires everything required by assignment 3 and assignment 4, plus the following.

Getting Started Tutorial

An end-user "getting started" tutorial, in the form of a screencast, for at least one of the main usage scenarios from your user manual.

Final Presentation

Your final presentation must be no shorter than 8 minutes and no longer than 10 minutes. Presentations that are too short or too long will be penalized! Use PowerPoint?, or a free equivalent, and design your presentation to be interesting and informative for both technical and non-technical audience members.

You must provide at least the following:

  • A clear one-sentence summary of your system that ordinary non-technical users could understand and find helpful.
  • Tell us what your system does in clear, non-technical language. What are its most interesting features?
  • An architectural diagram (not a class diagram!!) of your system. Summarize the basic system implementation details and design choices.
  • A discussion of the testing you did to ensure that the system works correctly. Don't bore us by listing dozens of low-level test cases. Instead, summarize all the testing you have done, and tell us why we should believe your system is error-free and works as promised.
  • How many known bugs do you have? Are there features you intended to implemented that you didn't?
  • List feedback --- good and bad --- from real users (i.e. people not on your team!) of your system. If you had no real users, then say so.
  • Finally, give a post-mortem analysis of your team and project, e.g.
    • What project management techniques worked well, and what worked not so well?
    • What were the major technical problems you encountered?
    • What were the major "human" problems you encountered while working as a team?
    • If you were to do the project over again, what would you do differently? What would you have done the same? What advice would you give to next year's students?
  • This is a formal presentation, and so it is expected to be substantially more polished than your previous presentations. Your group should do at least one practice run-through, and read general advice for giving oral presentations, such as this or perhaps this.

Presentation Times and Due Date

The final presentations will take place during the regular class times on Wednesday April 2nd, and Friday April 4th.

All team members are expected to be present both days, even if they are not presenting.
