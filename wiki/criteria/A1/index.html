Assignment 1: Project Plan and Website

This assignment has two parts: the creation of your team's project website, and preparing a scope and vision document.

Project Website

Create a project website to organize all your source code and documentation. Part of this website must be a publicly accessible Subversion (SVN) source code repository. You can easily get free software project hosting websites from, for example, Google Projet Hosting (http://code.google.com/hosting/), or sourceForge (http://sourceforge.net/). In past years, most teams have preferred Google Project Hosting, since it is easier to join. Keep in mind that these public hosting sites will make your code and documents available to the public.

In addition to your teammates, you must also add the course instructor and TA as full project members. They will not be doing any work for your team, of course: they need full access for marking purposes.

Your project website needs at least the following information:

  • Team name.

  • Full name, student number, and email address of each team member. Also include a brief technical biography of each team member, listing their relevant skills and interests.

  • The title of this assignment, and each future assignment, must be clearly displayed, e.g. in a title or as a menu item.

  • A table of contents linking to the 5 assignments. Provide "stub" pages for future assignments.

  • The project manager (PM) for this assignment, and for future assignments. Everyone in your group must be PM at least once.

    In addition to their other project work, the PM's has three extra responsibilities:

    • Organize meetings, and take notes.
    • Ensure that the assignment is ready for submission.
    • Create and give a presentation to the rest of the class. Of course, other team members can help with presentation, but the PM is in charge of putting it together and making sure it runs smoothly. The presentation should be semi-formal: prepare PowerPoint? (or equivalent) slides, but be prepared to answer questions about any part of your project or presentation.

  • Indicate under what open source license you will be releasing your source code. Include a link to the license.

  • Provide a link to your Subversion repository. Include explicit instructions for how to check out your project.

  • Give a detailed schedule of your team meetings: show the day, time, and location of each meeting. Take into account the CMPT 275 assignment and exam dates, other course due dates, known absences of team members (due to work, other classes, holidays, etc.), and so on.

  • Draw a diagram that shows all the documents and activities --- for this assignment and all future ones --- and they relate to each other. The purpose of this diagram is for your team to have a birds-eye view of the work you have to do. Do not clutter of up your diagram with unnecessary (for this diagram!) information such as dates or work assignments.

Make your website easy to read and navigate. Keep in mind that this assignment is not about designing a fancy website, so don't go overboard.

Vision and Scope Document

The vision and scope document is a good way to communicate the main goals of your project to team members and other stakeholders. Write it in non-technical language that any SFU student could understand.

Your team should schedule at least one brainstorming session to work out the scope and vision of your project. Do not worry about low-level details at this point (those come in later assignments), but always keep in mind that you are expected to implement everything this document proposes. Be sure to have someone make notes of this session! They will be very useful when writing your vision and scope document.

Structure the document as follows.

Problem Description

  1. Project background This summarizes the problem the project is aiming to solve, and discusses the basic goals at the highest level. This should not be too long or too short.
  2. Stakeholders List the people who have a stake in the success of the project.
  3. Users Describe the intended users of the system. Clearly state any assumptions you are making about their expertise, experience, or background.
  4. Risks List the risks your project faces. Organize them in a table with the following information:
    • The risk itself.
    • The likelihood of the risk occurring, e.g. low, medium, or high chance.
    • The severity of the risk, if it occurs, e.g. low, medium, high, or catastrophic.
  5. Assumptions List the important assumptions made by stakeholders, users, and team members.

Vision of Solution

  1. Vision statement Explain the project's purpose, and what it is expected to accomplish.
  2. Architectural Diagram Draw a "lines and boxes" diagram that names the major components of your system and how they relate. This is not a technical diagram: it is a simple visual overview of the structure of your system meant for non-technical readers.
  3. List of features List the major features of your system (about 10 or so). A feature is a small coherent unit of functionality. Each feature should have its own paragraph with an explicit name and description. The description need not be very detailed; a couple of sentences is fine.
  4. Scope of phased release Your project will be developed in three versions, and so in this section you list the expected functionality and features of each version.
  5. Features that will not be implemented List major features that are not going to be implemented, along with the reasons why.
